Beef Wellington

Recipe Information

Prep Time Cooking Time Servings
30 minutes 40-45 minutes 4-6 servings

Ingredients

For the Beef Wellington:

  • 2 pounds (900g) beef tenderloin
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 8-10 slices of prosciutto or Parma ham
  • 1 pound (450g) puff pastry
  • 2 egg yolks (for egg wash)

For the Mushroom Duxelles:

  • 1 pound (450g) mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 2 shallots, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup dry white wine
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream

Directions

STEP 1:Prepare the Mushroom Duxelles:

In a food processor, pulse the mushrooms until finely chopped.

STEP 2:

In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the shallots and garlic, sauté for a few minutes until translucent.

STEP 3:

Add the chopped mushrooms and cook until they release their moisture and it evaporates.

STEP 4:

Pour in the white wine and cook until it's mostly evaporated.

STEP 5:

Stir in the heavy cream and cook until the mixture thickens.

STEP 6:

Season with salt and pepper. Remove from heat and let it cool.

STEP 7:Prepare the Beef:

Season the beef tenderloin with salt and black pepper.

STEP 8:

Heat olive oil in a skillet over high heat. Sear the beef on all sides until nicely browned. Remove from heat and let it cool.

STEP 9:

Brush the seared beef with Dijon mustard.

STEP 10 :Wrap in Prosciutto:

Lay out a large piece of plastic wrap. Overlap the prosciutto slices on the plastic wrap and spread the mushroom duxelles over the prosciutto.

STEP 11:Wrap in Puff Pastry:

Place the seared beef on top of the mushroom-covered prosciutto.

STEP 12:

Use the plastic wrap to help roll the prosciutto and mushroom mixture around the beef.

STEP 13:

Wrap it tightly and refrigerate for about 30 minutes.

STEP 14:Preheat Oven:

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

STEP 15:Egg Wash and Bake:

Roll out the puff pastry on a floured surface to a size that will cover the beef.

STEP 16:

Place the wrapped beef in the center of the puff pastry and brush the edges of the pastry with egg yolks.

STEP 17:

Fold the pastry over the beef, sealing it completely. Trim any excess pastry and brush the entire pastry with more egg wash for a golden finish.

STEP 18:

Place it on a baking sheet seam side down.

STEP 19:Bake:

Bake in the preheated oven for about 30-40 minutes or until the pastry is golden brown and the beef reaches your desired level of doneness (use a meat thermometer for accuracy).

STEP 20:

Let it rest for a few minutes before slicing.

Cooking Tips and Tricks:

  • Use High-Quality Beef:Invest in a good-quality beef fillet as it's the star of the dish. Look for well-marbled, center-cut fillet for the best flavor and tenderness.

  • Properly Seal the Pastry:To prevent the pastry from getting soggy, make sure to seal it tightly around the beef. You can brush the beef with mustard or a duxelles (mushroom paste) before wrapping it in puff pastry for added flavor and to help with sealing.

  • Chill the Wellington:After assembling the Beef Wellington, it's essential to chill it in the refrigerator for at least 20-30 minutes. This helps set the pastry and maintain its shape during baking.

  • Make a Test Cut: Before serving, make a small cut in the pastry to check the doneness of the beef. This way, you can ensure it's cooked to your preferred level (rare, medium-rare, etc.) without overcooking.

  • Rest Before Slicing: Allow the Beef Wellington to rest for about 10 minutes after baking before slicing. This helps redistribute the juices within the meat, resulting in a juicier and more tender dish.
