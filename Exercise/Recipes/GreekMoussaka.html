Greek Moussaka

Recipe Information

Prep Time Cooking Time Servings
30-45 minutes 60-90 minutes 6-8 servings

Ingredients

For the Moussaka:

  • 2 large eggplants (aubergines), sliced lengthwise into approximately 1.3 cm-thick slices
  • 680 grams ground lamb or beef
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 400 grams crushed tomatoes (canned)
  • 120 ml red wine
  • 60 grams tomato paste
  • 5 grams ground cinnamon
  • 2.5 grams ground allspice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 60 ml olive oil, for frying the eggplant
  • 25 grams breadcrumbs

For the Bechamel Sauce:

  • 960 ml milk
  • 115 grams unsalted butter
  • 60 grams all-purpose flour
  • 0.6 grams ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 large eggs
  • 115 grams grated Parmesan cheese

Directions

STEP 1:

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

STEP 2:

Place the eggplant slices on a baking sheet and sprinkle both sides with salt. Let them sit for about 20 minutes to draw out any bitterness. Rinse and pat them dry with paper towels.

STEP 3:

Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Fry the eggplant slices until they are golden brown on both sides. Drain them on paper towels.

STEP 4:

In the same skillet, add the ground lamb or beef and cook until browned. Remove any excess fat.

STEP 5:

Add the chopped onion and garlic to the meat and cook until the onion is soft and translucent.

STEP 6:

Stir in the crushed tomatoes, red wine, tomato paste, ground cinnamon, ground allspice, salt, and pepper. Simmer for about 15 minutes until the sauce thickens.

STEP 7:

In a separate saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat for the bechamel sauce. Stir in the flour to create a roux. Cook for a few minutes until the roux is lightly browned.

STEP 8:

Gradually whisk in the milk, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Continue to cook and stir until the sauce thickens and becomes smooth.

STEP 9:

Remove the saucepan from the heat. Beat the eggs in a separate bowl and then gradually whisk them into the bechamel sauce.

STEP 10:

Spread a layer of breadcrumbs in the bottom of a greased baking dish.

STEP 11:

Place a layer of fried eggplant slices over the breadcrumbs

STEP 12:

Add a layer of the meat sauce on top of the eggplant.

STEP 13:

Repeat the layers until you've used all the ingredients, finishing with a layer of eggplant on top.

STEP 14:

Pour the bechamel sauce evenly over the top layer of eggplant.

STEP 15:

Sprinkle the grated Parmesan cheese over the bechamel.

STEP 16:

Bake the moussaka in the preheated oven for about 45 minutes or until it's golden brown and bubbling.

STEP 17:

Allow the moussaka to cool slightly before slicing and serving. Enjoy your homemade Greek Moussaka!

Cooking Tips and Tricks:

  • Eggplant Prep: After slicing the eggplants, lightly salt them and let them sit for about 15-20 minutes. This helps draw out excess moisture and bitterness. Pat them dry with paper towels before frying.

  • Frying Eggplant:When frying the eggplant slices, use a generous amount of olive oil, and make sure the oil is hot before adding the slices. Fry until they're golden brown on both sides. Place the fried slices on paper towels to remove excess oil.

  • Meat Seasoning:Season the ground lamb or beef generously with salt, pepper, and spices like cinnamon and allspice. These spices add a unique flavor to the dish.

  • Tomato Sauce: Simmer the crushed tomatoes, red wine, minced garlic, and tomato paste together to create a rich and flavorful tomato sauce. Let it reduce slightly.

  • Bechamel Sauce:When making the bechamel sauce, whisk continuously to avoid lumps. Cook until it thickens and becomes creamy. Season with nutmeg, salt, and pepper.

  • Layering:Assemble the moussaka by layering the eggplant, meat sauce, and bechamel sauce in a baking dish. Repeat the layers, ending with a layer of bechamel sauce on top.

  • Parmesan Cheese:Sprinkle the grated Parmesan cheese on top of the bechamel layer before baking. It will create a golden and cheesy crust.

  • Resting:Let the moussaka rest for a bit after baking before slicing and serving. This allows the layers to set and makes it easier to cut neat portions.

  • Serving: Moussaka is traditionally served warm but can also be enjoyed at room temperature. It often tastes even better the next day as the flavors meld together.
