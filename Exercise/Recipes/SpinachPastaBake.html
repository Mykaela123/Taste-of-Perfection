Creamy Lemon & Spinach Pasta Bake

Recipe Information

Prep Time Cooking Time Servings
30 minutes 60 minutes 6 servings

Ingredients

  • 340g whole-wheat penne or rotini
  • 113g reduced-fat cream cheese, softened
  • 1 tablespoon whole-grain mustard
  • 1 teaspoon grated lemon zest, plus more for garnish
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • ¼ teaspoon ground pepper, plus more for garnish
  • ¼ teaspoon salt, divided
  • 2 ¼ cups half-and-half milk
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • ¼ cup chopped scallions
  • 10 cups packed fresh spinach (about 10 ounces)
  • 1 teaspoon grated garlic
  • 1 cup shredded fontina cheese, divided
  • 3 tablespoons lemon juice

Directions

STEP 1:

Preheat oven to 177°C.Coat a 9-by-13-inch baking dish with cooking spray. Bring a large pot of water to a boil over high heat. Cook pasta according to package directions. Drain well in a colander. Wipe the pot clean.

STEP 2:

Meanwhile, whisk cream cheese, mustard, lemon zest, onion powder, pepper and 1/8 teaspoon salt in a medium bowl. Gradually whisk in half-and-half.

STEP 3:

Heat oil in the pot over medium-high heat. Add scallions and the remaining 1/8 teaspoon salt; cook, stirring constantly, until softened, about 3 minutes. Add spinach and garlic; cook, stirring constantly, until the spinach is bright green and wilted, 1 to 2 minutes. Remove from heat. Add the pasta, the cream cheese mixture and 1/2 cup fontina; stir to combine. Gently stir in lemon juice. Transfer to the prepared baking dish; top with the remaining 1/2 cup fontina. Cover tightly with foil. Bake until warmed through and slightly bubbling around the edges, 15 to 20 minutes. Garnish with additional pepper and lemon zest, if desired.

Cooking Tips and Tricks:

  • Fresh Ingredients:Use fresh spinach and lemons for the best flavor. Fresh spinach wilts nicely in the sauce, and freshly squeezed lemon juice adds a bright and zesty kick.

  • Precooked Pasta: To save time, you can use precooked pasta. However, if you prefer to cook your pasta from scratch, make sure to undercook it slightly, as it will continue cooking in the oven.

  • Flavor Layering Build flavors by seasoning each component separately. Season the spinach, the sauce, and the pasta to ensure that every element is well-seasoned.

  • Creamy Sauce: For a truly creamy sauce, use heavy cream or half-and-half. If you want a lighter version, you can use milk, but the sauce won't be as rich.

  • Cheese Selection: Experiment with different cheeses like Parmesan, mozzarella, or Fontina to vary the flavor profile of your dish.

  • Fresh Herbs: Consider adding fresh herbs like basil or parsley for an extra layer of flavor and a pop of color.

  • Baking Time:Keep an eye on your pasta bake while it's in the oven. You want a beautifully golden crust, but avoid overbaking, which can dry out the dish.

  • Pairing: Serve it with a simple salad or garlic bread to complement the flavors and add some freshness to the meal.
