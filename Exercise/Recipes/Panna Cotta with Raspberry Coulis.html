Panna Cotta with Raspberry Coulis:

Recipe Information

Prep Time Cooking Time Servings
20 minutes Panna Cotta: 4 hours (chilling time) Raspberry Coulis: 10 minutes (plus cooling time) 4 servings

Ingredients

For the Panna Cotta:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons powdered gelatin
  • 3 tablespoons cold water

For the Raspberry Coulis:

  • 2 cups fresh or frozen raspberries
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Directions

For the Panna Cotta:

STEP 1:

In a small bowl, sprinkle the powdered gelatin over cold water. Let it sit for about 5 minutes to bloom.

STEP 2:

In a saucepan, heat the heavy cream, whole milk, and sugar over medium heat. Stir until the sugar dissolves and the mixture is hot but not boiling.

STEP 3:

Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract.

STEP 4:

Add the bloomed gelatin to the cream mixture and stir until completely dissolved.

STEP 5:

Pour the mixture into serving glasses or ramekins.

STEP 6:

Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or until set.

For the Raspberry Coulis:

STEP 1:

In a blender or food processor, combine the raspberries, granulated sugar, and lemon juice.

STEP 2:

Blend until smooth.

STEP 3:

Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl to remove the seeds.

STEP 4:

Refrigerate the coulis until ready to serve.

To Serve:

STEP 1:

When the Panna Cotta has set, spoon the raspberry coulis over the top.

STEP 2:

Garnish with fresh raspberries or mint leaves, if desired.

Cooking Tips and Tricks:

  • Be sure not to boil the cream mixture for the Panna Cotta; it should be heated just until hot.

  • You can use silicone molds for fun shapes or traditional ramekins for classic presentation.

  • To unmold the Panna Cotta easily, dip the ramekin or mold into hot water for a few seconds before inverting it onto a plate.

  • Adjust the sweetness of the raspberry coulis to your liking by adding more or less sugar.

  • Fresh raspberries make a delightful garnish, but other berries or fruit can also be used to complement the dessert's flavor.
