Asian Spring Rolls

Recipe Information

Prep Time Cooking Time Servings
30 minutes 30-45 minutes 4-6 servings

Ingredients

For the spring rolls

  • 8-10 rice paper spring roll wrappers
  • 1 cup cooked and peeled shrimp, sliced in half lengthwise (optional)
  • 1 cup cooked vermicelli rice noodles
  • 1 cup fresh lettuce leaves
  • 1 cup fresh bean sprouts
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro leaves
  • 1/2 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1 small cucumber, julienned
  • 1 small carrot, julienned
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts, chopped (optional)

For the dipping sauce

  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
  • Red pepper flakes or Sriracha sauce for some heat (optional)

Directions

STEP 1:

Prepare all the filling ingredients-slice the shrimp in half lengthwise, julienne the cucumber and carrot, and have the fresh herbs, lettuce, and bean sprouts ready.

STEP 2:

Fill a large shallow bowl with warm water. Dip one rice paper wrapper into the water for about 10-15 seconds until it becomes soft and pliable. Be careful not to soak it for too long, or it may tear.

STEP 3:

Lay the softened rice paper wrapper on a clean, damp kitchen towel or a plate.

STEP 4:

Assemble the spring roll by placing a few leaves of lettuce, a small amount of vermicelli noodles, some shrimp (if using), herbs, cucumber, carrot, and bean sprouts in the center of the wrapper.

STEP 5:

Fold the sides of the wrapper over the filling, then fold the bottom part of the wrapper over the filling and roll it tightly. Think of it like folding in the sides and then rolling it up like a burrito.

STEP 6:

Repeat the process with the remaining wrappers and filling ingredients.

STEP 7:

To make the dipping sauce, combine all the sauce ingredients in a small bowl and mix well. Adjust the spiciness to your liking with red pepper flakes or Sriracha.

STEP 8:

Serve the spring rolls with the dipping sauce on the side and garnish with chopped roasted peanuts if desired.

Cooking Tips and Tricks:

  • Use Fresh Ingredients:Ensure that all your ingredients, including vegetables, herbs, and proteins, are fresh and of high quality for the best flavor and texture.

  • Soak Rice Paper Correctly: When working with rice paper wrappers, dip them in warm water just until they become pliable but not too soft. Over-soaking can make them too sticky and challenging to handle.

  • Layer Ingredients Smartly: Layer your ingredients strategically to ensure even distribution of flavors and textures in each bite. Start with a lettuce leaf or rice noodles, followed by herbs, vegetables, and proteins.

  • Don't Overfill:Avoid overfilling the spring rolls, as it can make them difficult to roll and may cause them to tear. Use moderation to achieve a well-balanced filling.

  • Rolling Technique:Roll the spring rolls tightly, folding in the sides as you go, to create a neat and compact package. Practice makes perfect, so don't be discouraged if your first ones aren't perfect.

  • Damp Towel:Place a damp kitchen towel over the prepared spring rolls to prevent them from drying out while you work on assembling the others.

  • Serve with Dipping Sauce:Serve the spring rolls with a flavorful dipping sauce, such as a peanut sauce, sweet chili sauce, or hoisin sauce, for an extra layer of taste.

  • Customize to Taste: Feel free to customize your spring rolls with your favorite ingredients. You can add cooked shrimp, tofu, or other proteins for variety.

  • Serve Chilled: Spring rolls are best served chilled, so refrigerate them for at least 30 minutes before serving to enhance their flavors.
