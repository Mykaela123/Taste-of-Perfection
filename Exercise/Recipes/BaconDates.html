Bacon-wrapped dates with almond and cream cheese filling

Recipe Information

Prep Time Cooking Time Servings
15 minutes 20-25 minutes 4-6 servings

Ingredients

  • 12 Toothpicks (for securing the bacon)
  • 6 slices of bacon, cut in half crosswise
  • 1/2 cup or 12 pieces of cream cheese
  • 12 whole almonds
  • 12 dates, pitted

Directions

STEP 1:

Preheat your oven to 190°C.

STEP 2:

Make a small slit in each date to create an opening for stuffing. Be careful not to cut the date in half completely.

STEP 3:

Take a whole almond and stuff it into the opening of each date. Then, use a small spoon or a piping bag to fill the dates with cream cheese. You can also mix the almond and cream cheese together before stuffing if you prefer.

STEP 4:

Take a half-slice of bacon and wrap it around each stuffed date, securing it with a toothpick. Make sure to wrap the bacon tightly around the date.

STEP 5:

Place the bacon-wrapped dates on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper or a wire rack placed on top of a baking sheet. This will allow excess grease to drip away during baking

STEP 6:

Bake the dates in the preheated oven for about 15-20 minutes or until the bacon is crispy and cooked to your liking. Keep an eye on them to prevent overcooking.

STEP 7:

Allow the bacon-wrapped dates to cool slightly before serving. The cream cheese filling will be hot, so be cautious when taking a bite. Serve them as a tasty appetizer for your guests to enjoy.

Cooking Tips and Tricks:

  • Use Fresh and Plump Dates:Choose fresh and plump Medjool dates for the best flavor and texture. They should be soft and slightly sticky to the touch.

  • Pit the Dates Carefully:When making the slit in the dates to remove the pits, be gentle to avoid splitting them in half completely. You want to create a pocket for the filling.

  • Soak Toothpicks:If you're using wooden toothpicks, consider soaking them in water for about 15-30 minutes before using. This helps prevent them from burning in the oven.

  • Secure the Bacon Well: Ensure that the bacon is tightly wrapped around each date and secured with a toothpick. This prevents the bacon from unraveling during cooking.

  • Partial Pre-cook the Bacon: If you prefer crispier bacon, you can partially pre-cook it in the oven for a few minutes before wrapping the dates. This helps ensure that the bacon gets crispy without overcooking the dates.

  • Experiment with Fillings:Get creative with the fillings. While cream cheese and almonds are classic choices, you can try other ingredients like blue cheese, pecans, or even jalapeño slices for a spicy kick.

  • Rack or Parchment Paper: Placing the wrapped dates on a wire rack over a baking sheet or using parchment paper helps elevate them, allowing excess grease to drip away, resulting in crispier bacon.

  • Monitor Cooking Time:Keep a close eye on the dates while they're in the oven to prevent overcooking. Bacon can go from undercooked to overcooked quickly, so adjust the cooking time as needed.

  • Pairing Suggestions: Consider serving these bacon-wrapped dates with a drizzle of honey or a balsamic reduction for an extra burst of flavor.
