No-Bake Lemon-Blueberry Cheesecake Bars

Recipe Information

Prep Time Cooking Time Servings
20 minutes 120 minutes 16 servings

Ingredients

  • 7 ounces graham crackers (13 sheets), broken into larger pieces
  • ½ cup pecans, toasted (see Tip)
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ⅓ cup canola oil
  • 2 (8 ounce) packages reduced-fat cream cheese, softened
  • 2 cups nonfat plain strained yogurt, such as Greek-style
  • ⅔ cup confectioners' sugar
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons lemon curd
  • 4 cups fresh blueberries

Directions

STEP 1:

Add cream cheese, yogurt, confectioners' sugar, lemon zest, lemon juice and vanilla to the food processor.

STEP 2:

Puree until smooth, about 1 minute.

STEP 3:

Dollop the mixture over the crust, then gently spread into an even layer.

STEP 4:

Cover and refrigerate until cold, at least 2 hours and up to 1 day.

STEP 5:

Place lemon curd in a large microwave-safe bowl; microwave on High until warmed, about 20 seconds.

STEP 6:

Add blueberries to the bowl; toss to gently combine. Spoon over the cheesecake. Cut into 16 squares.

Cooking Tips and Tricks:

  • Toasting Pecans: When toasting pecans (or any nuts), do so in a dry skillet over medium-low heat. Stir them frequently and remove from heat as soon as they become fragrant. This enhances their flavor.

  • Preparing the Graham Cracker Crust: Ensure that the graham crackers are finely crushed and mixed thoroughly with the canola oil and a pinch of salt. Press the mixture firmly into the pan to create a solid, even crust.

  • Soften Cream Cheese:Soften cream cheese by leaving it at room temperature for about 30 minutes. This makes it easier to blend and results in a smoother cheesecake filling.

  • Using Greek-Style Yogurt:

  • Lemon Zest:When zesting a lemon, be careful not to include the bitter white pith. Use a fine grater or zester for the best results.

  • Lemon Curd Drizzle: Warm the lemon curd slightly in the microwave to make it easier to drizzle over the cheesecake bars. This adds an extra burst of lemony flavor.

  • Chilling Time:Allow the cheesecake bars to chill in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours (or up to 1 day) before serving. This helps them set properly and develop better flavor.

  • Cutting and Serving:To create clean and neat squares, use a sharp knife dipped in hot water, then wiped dry before each cut. This prevents the bars from sticking and ensures clean edges.
