Tiramisu

Recipe Information

Prep Time Cooking Time Servings
30 minutes 30 minutes (plus chilling time). 8 servings

Ingredients

  • 6 egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup mascarpone cheese
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 1/2 cups brewed espresso, cooled
  • 2 tablespoons coffee liqueur (optional)
  • 1 package (about 7 ounces) ladyfinger cookies
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder, for dusting
  • Dark chocolate shavings, for garnish
  • 1 tablespoon unsweetened cocoa powder (for dusting)

Directions

STEP 1:

In a heatproof bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and sugar until thick and pale. Place the bowl over a pot of simmering water, making sure the bottom of the bowl doesn't touch the water. Continue to whisk until the mixture reaches 160°F (71°C). Remove from heat and let it cool.

STEP 2:

Once the egg mixture has cooled, add the mascarpone cheese and mix until smooth.

STEP 3:

In a separate bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form. Gently fold the whipped cream into the mascarpone mixture until fully combined.

STEP 4:

In a shallow dish, combine the brewed espresso and coffee liqueur (if using). Dip each ladyfinger into the espresso mixture for a few seconds, making sure not to soak them too long, as they can become too soggy.

STEP 5:

Arrange a layer of dipped ladyfingers in the bottom of a serving dish or individual glasses.

STEP 6:

Spread half of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers.

STEP 7:

Repeat with another layer of dipped ladyfingers and the remaining mascarpone mixture.

STEP 8:

Cover and refrigerate the tiramisu for at least 4 hours or overnight to allow the flavors to meld.

STEP 9:

Before serving, dust the top with unsweetened cocoa powder and garnish with dark chocolate shavings.

Cooking Tips and Tricks:

  • Use fresh, high-quality ingredients, especially for the espresso and mascarpone cheese, as they greatly influence the flavor of the Tiramisu.

  • Be cautious when dipping the ladyfingers into the espresso mixture; a quick dip is ideal to maintain a balanced texture.

  • Chill the Tiramisu for several hours or overnight. This allows the flavors to meld and the dessert to set properly.

  • When dusting with cocoa powder, you can use a fine-mesh sieve for a uniform, light coating.

  • Feel free to customize your Tiramisu by adding a sprinkle of cocoa, grated chocolate, or a drizzle of coffee liqueur on top for extra flavor and presentation.

  • Serve chilled for the best taste and texture. Enjoy this classic Italian dessert!
