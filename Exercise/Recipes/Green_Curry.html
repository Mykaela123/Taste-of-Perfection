Thai: Green Curry with Vegetables and Tofu

Recipe Information

Prep Time Cooking Time Servings
15 minutes 25-30 minutes 4 servings

Ingredients

For the Green Curry Paste:

  • 2-3 green Thai bird's eye chilies (adjust for spice level)
  • 2 shallots, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, sliced (white part only)
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of galangal or ginger, peeled and sliced
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon shrimp paste (optional, for non-vegetarian)
  • Zest from 1 lime
  • 2-3 fresh kaffir lime leaves (or use lime zest)
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro leaves and stems
  • 1/4 cup fresh Thai basil leaves (or regular basil)
  • Salt, to taste

For the Curry:

  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 2-3 tablespoons green curry paste (adjust for spice level)
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (bell peppers, carrots, zucchini, etc.), sliced
  • 1 cup firm tofu, cubed
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1-2 teaspoons brown sugar or palm sugar
  • 2-3 tablespoons fish sauce or soy sauce (for vegetarian/vegan)
  • Fresh Thai basil leaves for garnish (optional)
  • Cooked jasmine rice for serving

Directions

STEP 1:Make the Green Curry Paste:

In a food processor, combine all the green curry paste ingredients and blend until you have a smooth paste. Adjust the spiciness by adding more or fewer chilies.

STEP 2:Prepare the Curry:

In a large pan or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the green curry paste and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until fragrant.

STEP 3:Add the Tofu and Vegetables:

Add the cubed tofu and sliced vegetables to the pan. Stir-fry for about 5 minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender.

STEP 4:Add Coconut Milk:

Pour in the coconut milk and bring the mixture to a gentle simmer. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.

STEP 5:Season the Curry:

Season the curry with brown sugar and fish sauce (or soy sauce for a vegetarian/vegan version). Adjust the seasoning to your taste. If you prefer a sweeter curry, add more sugar.

STEP 6:Simmer:

Let the curry simmer for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables are cooked to your liking and the tofu is heated through.

STEP 7:Serve:

Serve the green curry over cooked jasmine rice. Garnish with fresh Thai basil leaves if desired.

Cooking Tips and Tricks:

  • Choose High-Quality Green Curry Paste:The key to a flavorful green curry is the curry paste. Look for a good quality Thai green curry paste from a trusted brand or consider making your own for an authentic taste.

  • Tofu Preparation:If using tofu, it's a good idea to press it first to remove excess moisture. This will help the tofu absorb the flavors of the curry better. Cut it into bite-sized pieces and pan-fry until lightly golden before adding to the curry.

  • Variety of Vegetables:Feel free to customize the vegetables to your liking. Popular choices include bell peppers, bamboo shoots, zucchini, and eggplant. Use what's in season or what you have on hand.

  • Coconut Milk:Use full-fat coconut milk for a creamy and rich curry. Shake the can before opening to ensure the coconut cream and milk are well mixed

  • Balance the Flavors:Thai curries are known for their balance of sweet, salty, spicy, and sour flavors. Adjust the amount of green curry paste, sugar, and lime juice to suit your taste preferences.

  • Simmering Time: Allow the curry to simmer for a few minutes to meld the flavors, but be cautious not to overcook the vegetables. They should be tender-crisp

  • Serve with Accompaniments: Serve your Thai Green Curry with steamed jasmine rice or rice noodles. You can also offer condiments like fish sauce, chili flakes, or extra lime wedges on the side for diners to adjust the flavors to their liking.
